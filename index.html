By Lokesh Wadgaonkar | Tier 2/3 College → IIT M.Tech → EV/Energy Startup

🔥 The Non-Negotiable Rules

  1. From Sem 2: Solve at least 5 GATE previous year questions every single day. No exceptions.
  2. From Sem 4: Maintain a GATE error notebook. Every wrong answer gets analyzed.
  3. From Sem 5: Give one full mock test every Sunday. Treat it like the real exam.
  4. Every semester: Build one project that uses your GATE knowledge. This is your startup portfolio.
  5. No zero days: Even 30 minutes = 5 GATE math problems. Momentum > intensity.
  6. Daily grind: 5–6 hours self-study on top of college. Consistency beats intensity.

💡 General Tips for the Grind

  1. 🔴 Prioritize GATE subjects first — they are your ticket to IIT M.Tech.
  2. 🟡 Startup skills second — they differentiate you in M.Tech interviews and build your startup portfolio.
  3. 🟢 Projects bridge both — every project should use GATE knowledge + startup tools.
  4. Consistency beats intensity — 5 hours daily for 4 years beats 12-hour cram sessions.
  5. Build a portfolio — Maintain a GitHub repository + blog documenting every project.
  6. Use NPTEL heavily — Free, taught by IIT professors, GATE-relevant.
  7. Previous year papers are king — Solve every GATE EE paper from 2000 to 2025. Twice.
  8. Competitions — e-Yantra (IIT Bombay), Smart India Hackathon, TI Innovation Challenge.
  9. Buy components smartly — Robu.in, Amazon, Element14, JLCPCB for PCBs.
  10. Never skip Math — 15% weightage. Do it daily from Day 1.

⚠️ The Brutal Truth

This schedule is tight. You will miss parties, festivals, some college events. Your friends will call you boring.

But from a Tier 2/3 college, this is the only path to an IIT M.Tech + a startup-ready skillset. There are no shortcuts.

4 years of consistency = AIR 100–500. Slack off in Sem 1 or 2 = scrambling in Sem 7 like everyone else.
